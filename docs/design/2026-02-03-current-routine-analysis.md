## Problems Identified

### 1. Excessive Volume Per Session
- **Back day: 10 exercises** - Research shows diminishing returns after 3-4 exercises per muscle group
- **Total per-session fatigue:** "Tiring collectively" - indicates junk volume accumulation
- **Energy depletion:** "If I still have juice left" approach leads to inconsistent training

### 2. Low Frequency Per Muscle
- **1x per week per muscle** - Beginners can train each muscle 3x/week due to faster recovery
- **Long recovery windows:** Back sits idle for 7 days after 30 sets of punishment
- **Slower progress:** "Getting stronger but slowly" - likely due to infrequent stimulus

### 3. Missing Muscle Groups
- **No dedicated core work** - Critical for lower back health (user's weakness)
- **No posterior chain emphasis** - Glutes, hamstrings undertrained (weak legs concern)
- **No mobility work** - Stiff shoulders need dedicated attention

### 4. Unbalanced Loading Patterns
- **Back: 30 sets/week** vs **Triceps: 6 sets/week** - Massive imbalance
- **Calves: 6 sets** (2 exercises) - Good consideration for soleus/gastrocnemius, but isolated once weekly
- **Shoulders: Very light weights (2.5-5kg)** - Indicates weakness or mobility limitation (stiff shoulders)

### 5. Training to Failure
- **45-degree hyperextension @ RIR 0** - Training to failure on lower back exercise (risky for weak lower back)
- **"If I still have juice left"** - No structured progression, just fatigue accumulation

### Evidence-Based Optimization Principles

#### Muscle Fiber Type Considerations
Research shows different muscles have varying fiber type compositions:

**Slow-Twitch Dominant (Type I - Endurance):**
- Soleus (80-90% Type I) - Seated calf raises, slower tempo
- Erector Spinae/Lower Back (60% Type I) - Hyperextensions, controlled tempo
- Core muscles (55-60% Type I) - Planks, dead bugs, time under tension

**Mixed Fiber (50/50):**
- Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes - Moderate tempo, variety
- Lats, Traps - Rows and pulldowns, controlled
- Chest, Shoulders - Presses, moderate tempo

**Fast-Twitch Dominant (Type II - Power):**
- Triceps (60% Type II) - Explosive concentric, controlled eccentric
- Calves gastrocnemius (50-55% Type II) - Standing calf raises, explosive

**Training Implications:**
- Slow-twitch muscles: Longer time under tension (3-5 sec eccentric)
- Fast-twitch muscles: Explosive concentric (1 sec up), controlled eccentric (2-3 sec down)
- Mixed muscles: Moderate tempo (2 sec concentric, 2-3 sec eccentric)
